Invasive Plants A-Z
Cape Ivy
Cape ivy is an invasive plant from South Africa and was introduced to America in the 1800s. This plant grows in wetlands and streams.
Foxglove
Foxglove is native to Europe, Western Asia, and Northwestern Africa. Foxgloves are biennial flowers that grow in the shade.
Giant Knotweed
Giant knotweed is the largest of the three knotweeds and grow in moist areas such as riverbanks.
Lily of the Valley
Lilies of the Valley are native to Northern Europe and Asia. Lilies of the Valley grow in partial shade.
Ox-Eye Daisy
Ox-eye daisies are native to Europe and Asia. Ox-eye daisies grow in forests and many plant communities.
Radish
Radishes grow in grasslands and open areas. They are also sometimes found in wetlands and are toxic to livestock.
